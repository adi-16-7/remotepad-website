Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 7, 2026

The short version: RemotePad has no backend. We don't collect, store, or transmit your personal data, keystrokes, mouse movements, or any other usage information. Everything stays between your iPhone and your Mac on your local network.

This policy explains what data RemotePad ("the app", "we") handles, in plain English. If anything is unclear, write to remotepad.feedback@gmail.com and we'll happily explain.

What we do not collect

  • Your keystrokes, mouse movements, or scroll events. These travel directly from your iPhone to your Mac over your local network. They never reach a server we control.
  • Your IP address. The app uses Apple's Network framework to discover your Mac on the local network via Bonjour. Your IP is only seen by your own router.
  • Personal information. The app does not ask for, store, or transmit your name, email, contacts, photos, location, microphone, or camera data.
  • Analytics or tracking. We do not embed any third-party analytics SDKs. We do not use advertising IDs.

What is stored on your device

  • A pairing secret is generated when you pair an iPhone with a Mac. It's stored in the iOS Keychain on your iPhone and the macOS Keychain on your Mac. It is used only to sign and verify messages exchanged between your devices. You can clear it at any time via Settings → "Forget all Macs" (iPhone) or by deleting and reinstalling either app.
  • App settings (preferred tab, dark mode, debug toggles) are stored in iOS UserDefaults / macOS UserDefaults. These never leave your device.

Apple's standard frameworks

If you opt in to Share App Analytics in your iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements, Apple may share aggregated, anonymous crash reports and basic usage statistics with us via Apple's App Store Connect. We use this only to fix crashes. You can turn it off at any time. We never receive personally identifiable information through this channel.

In-App Purchases

If you purchase RemotePad Pro, the transaction is handled by Apple's StoreKit. We receive notification that the purchase succeeded but never see your payment method, billing address, or any other financial information.

Permissions we ask for

  • Local Network (iOS): so the app can discover your Mac via Bonjour. Used only on your local network.
  • Accessibility (macOS): so RemoteHost can move the cursor and inject keystrokes on your Mac when your iPhone sends them.
  • Automation → System Events (macOS): so the Mission Control and switch-spaces buttons in the iPhone app can trigger the corresponding system shortcuts.

You can revoke any of these permissions at any time from System Settings on the respective device.

Communications

If you email us at remotepad.feedback@gmail.com, we'll see the email address you wrote from. We use that solely to reply to you. We don't add you to any mailing list.

Children's privacy

RemotePad is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect data from children, because — as stated above — we do not collect data at all.

Changes to this policy

If we ever start collecting any data (for example, if we add cloud-based features in the future), we will update this policy and prominently notify existing users via an in-app banner before the change takes effect. Until you see such a notice, you can assume this policy is current.

Contact

Questions, concerns, or corrections: remotepad.feedback@gmail.com.
