Common questions.

Does it work over the internet?

No — RemotePad is local-network only. Both your iPhone and your Mac must be on the same Wi-Fi. This keeps it simple, fast, and private. If you need remote control over the internet, dedicated tools like Apple Remote Desktop, Screens, or Jump Desktop are better fits.

Why isn't the Mac app on the Mac App Store?

The Mac App Store requires sandboxing, which blocks the Accessibility and Automation permissions our app needs to move the mouse, type keystrokes, and trigger Mission Control. Several great utilities — Rectangle, Karabiner, BetterTouchTool — distribute the same way for the same reason.

Is it safe to install a Mac app from outside the App Store?

RemoteHost is signed with our Apple Developer ID and notarized by Apple — the same level of trust Apple gives to apps from indie developers like Panic and Iconfactory. macOS will verify the signature on first launch and won't show any "unidentified developer" warning.

What permissions does the Mac app need?

Accessibility so it can move the cursor and type keystrokes on your behalf. Automation (System Events) so the trackpad's Mission Control / Switch Spaces buttons work. The first launch walks you through both.

What do I get with Pro?

Media controls (play/pause, volume, mute), window-switching buttons (Mission Control, Switch Spaces), multiple Mac pairings, and any future Pro features. One-time $4.99. No subscription.

Can I use it with multiple Macs?

Yes — with Pro. The free tier remembers a single pairing; Pro remembers as many as you want and lets you switch between them.

Where do I send feedback or report a bug?

Email us at remotepad.feedback@gmail.com or use the Send Feedback button in the iOS app's Settings — it pre-fills the version and device info so we can help faster.
